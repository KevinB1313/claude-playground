Ancient China
2100–221 BCE
The foundational era of the Xia, Shang, and Zhou dynasties gave the world oracle bones, magnificent bronze culture, and the Mandate of Heaven. This period saw Confucius, Laozi, and the flowering of the Hundred Schools of Thought — philosophy that would shape all of East Asia.

Imperial Unification
221–206 BCE
Qin Shi Huang ended four centuries of warring states and forged the first unified Chinese empire in just 15 years. His legacy is monumental — the Great Wall, the awe-inspiring Terracotta Army, and sweeping standardizations of writing, weights, and currency that bound a fractured land into one civilization.

Han & Tang Golden Ages
206 BCE–907 CE
The Han dynasty established the Silk Road and Confucianism as state ideology, while Tang China became the most cosmopolitan civilization on earth. Chang'an, the Tang capital, was the world's largest city. Buddhism transformed Chinese spirituality, Tang poetry reached sublime heights, and Wu Zetian became China's only female emperor.

Song, Yuan & Ming
960–1644 CE
Song China gave the world printing, gunpowder, the compass, and paper money — the Four Great Inventions that transformed global history. The Mongol Yuan dynasty brought China into a vast Eurasian empire. Ming China then reasserted its grandeur with Zheng He's oceanic expeditions and the construction of the magnificent Forbidden City.

Qing Dynasty
1644–1912 CE
The Manchu-founded Qing dynasty expanded China to its greatest territorial extent, ruling over 400 million people at its peak. Yet the 19th century brought devastating humiliation — the Opium Wars forced open treaty ports, foreign powers carved out spheres of influence, and a crumbling imperial system finally collapsed in 1912.

Modern China
1912–present
After imperial collapse, China endured the Republic, warlord chaos, and brutal Japanese invasion before Mao Zedong's Communist Revolution in 1949. The Cultural Revolution tore society apart. Then Deng Xiaoping's pragmatic reforms launched the most rapid economic rise in human history, transforming China into a 21st-century superpower.
